Climate Change News

Greenhouse Gas

Greenhouse gas is any gas that is capable of absorbing infrared radiation (net heat energy) emitted from the Earth's surface and reradiating it back to the Earth's surface.

Ozone Depletion

Ozone depletion is the gradual thinning of the Earth's ozone layer in the upper atmosphere caused by the release of chemical compounds containing gaseous chlorine or bromine from industry and other human activities.

Global Warming

Global warming is the increase of the average air temperature near the surface of the Earth over the past one to two centuries due to the influence of human activities. This increase is affecting temperatures, precipitation and storms. It is also influencing ocean currents and atmospheric chemical composition to significantly alter weather events.

Carbon Footprint

Carbon footprint is the amount of carbon dioxide (CO2) emissions associated with all the activities of a person, building, corporation, country, etc. It includes direct emissions from those entities along with emissions produced by other entities who provide/produce services/goods that are consumed. Fossil fuels and greenhouse gases such as methane, nitrous oxide and chlorofluorocarbons are examples of problematic emissions.

Deforestation

Deforestation is the clearing or thinning of forests by humans. Forests are important factor in absorbing carbon dioxide from the atmosphere. As such, the reduction in forests contributes to greenhouse gas emission issues and global warming.
